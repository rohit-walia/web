THE COST OF ASPIRATION IS SACRIFICE
My University years were some of my toughest times endured because I had set a goal which many students could not achieve. My goal was to graduate debt free and without any financial assistance. This goal was tough because I started with absolutely nothing and in order to achieve this goal I would have to make a few sacrifices and work really really hard... and boy, was I determined.

I worked full-time overnight shifts for an airline catering company as a billing analyst. Although it was shift work, I asked to be permanently placed in nightshift so I could attend my schooling during the day. During these nightshifts, I developed my very first software solution to time consuming manual processes within the department. My program was essentially doing 50% of my job requirements in the time span of a few minutes and that allowed me to be more productive.

After some number crunching, I realized my sole income wasn't enough to pay the enormous tuition fee's so I started looking for an on-campus student job. It wasn't long until I was hired part-time as a Digital Communications Assistant. With income coming from both jobs and many sacrifices, I was able to graduate debt free.

On multiple occasions I got asked "What's your secret to managing all this?" Answer: The opportunity is available for any abled body but at a cost: you may have to sacrifice sleep, family time, & birthday parties; You may have to settle for the 3-hour nap in the backseat of your car vs 1/2 hour if you had driven home; You may have to settle for street food instead of home cooked meals. People already know hard work pays off but what they don't know lies beyond just effort: success demands sacrifices.
