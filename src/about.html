THE NEWSPAPER BOY
I landed my very first job as a newspaper carrier for 'The Guardian'. It didn't pay much but for a 14 year old kid, it meant everything! Hand delivering my neighbourhood newspapers was the foundational building blocks of my professional skillset. I honed my administrative skills from converting 50lbs of disorganized paper into packaged newspaper ready for distribution. I got creative and innovative as I expanded from delivering 100 houses to 200, eventually 300 houses.
WHEN THINGS GETS TOUGH, YOU GET TOUGHER
During my time working overnights as a billing analyst in the logistics department for an airline catering company, I developed software that automated time consuming manual processes. My program was essentially doing 50% of my job requirements which allowed me to sneak in an hour nap, essential for when I would have to go straight to school and attend multiple 3-hour lectures. Friends always asked me "how did you manage a full-time overnights job plus full-time schooling?" Answer: You make it happen. Its not easy, I sacrificed a majority of my sleep, family time, and friends birthdays; however, I was determined to graduate debt free.
DON'T SETTLE FOR COMFORT UNTIL YOU'VE MADE IT
One of the magnificant attributes of the human mind and body is it's ability to adapt. After a year of working full time overnights at Gate Gourmet and being a full time student, I realized I had adapted to this stressful schedule. What I thought was overwhelming in the beginning, was no longer anymore. With every iteration, the amount of effort I was outputting was decreasing and my productivity and efficieny was increasing. I think a Bruce Lee quote makes this point beautifully:

"I fear not the man who has practiced 10,000 kicks once, but I fear the man who had practiced one kick 10,000 times".

IM JUST GETTING STARTED
I remember the moment I had graduated from York University. I was writing two important letters, one with excitment and one with sorrow. My first letter was a notice of resignation and the other was a thank you letter. I was excited to exit Gate Gourmet to persue my career in the tech industry and I was sad to leave my part-time campus job simpily because I met some amazing people and a manager who believed in me. All in all, I had acomplished one of my milestones in life, namely to graduate debt free without anyone's help.
